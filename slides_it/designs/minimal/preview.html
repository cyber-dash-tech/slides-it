Three principles

01

Restraint

Every element must earn its place on the canvas. When in doubt, remove it. The strongest slides are the ones with the fewest distractions competing for attention.

02

Clarity

One idea per slide. Give the audience time to absorb each concept before moving on. Complexity belongs in the speaker's notes, not on the screen.

03

Rhythm

Typography sets the pace of understanding. Serif headings anchor each slide, sans-serif body text flows beneath. White space is punctuation — it tells the eye where to pause.

Key finding

Typography creates hierarchy without decoration

The contrast between DM Serif Display headings and DM Sans body text establishes a natural visual order. No colors, borders, or effects needed.

This principle is rooted in centuries of print design — the relationship between typefaces tells the reader what matters most, without a single pixel of decoration.

Evidence

  • Serif vs sans-serif creates instant visual hierarchy
  • Weight contrast (400 vs 300) guides the eye naturally
  • Generous line-height aids long-form reading comprehension
  • Letter-spacing on headings creates a sense of gravitas
